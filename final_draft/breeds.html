Popular Domestic Cats


Abyssinians are beloved by owners for their almost dog-like desire to socialize and follow their people around the house. They're blessed with high intelligence and what seems like a motor that runs almost nonstop, making them natural explorers.

American Bobtails are loving and incredibly intelligent cats possessing a distinctive wild appearance. They are extremely interactive cats that bond with their human family with great devotion.

The American Shorthair is a breed that’s completely devoted to its owner. They’re incredibly adaptable animals that do great with other pets, making them one of the best felines for households that already have other pets.

Balinese cats are known for being very talkative; expect yours to communicate with you about everything. These cats rarely stop being vocal, and they’re very sensitive to your speaking as well. A harsh tone can instantly hurt their feelings.


Popular Exotic Cats






The impressive Chausie was created by mixing the Abyssinian with wild jungle cats. Chausies have a high prey drive, so they don’t work well with smaller pets. Aside from needing constant company, these cats can be very adventurous and playful. If you buy from a breeder, Chausies typically range from $1,000 to $2,500 per kitten.

The elegantly wild Bengal cat is the product of mixing domesticated cats like the Egyptian Mau with the Asian Leopard cat. Bangles are known for their highly charismatic personalities. They are brilliant and extremely active. A healthy Bengal needs space to run, play, climb, and claw at their leisure. If you purchase a Bengal, you can expect to pay in the ballpark of $1,500 to $3,000 per kitten.

Savannahs were developed by crossing a Sevral with many common household cats. Savannah cats adore water, so you might find one accompanying you to the shower or trying to play in your dishwater. These cats are incredibly loyal and attentive. If you’re looking to buy a Savannah kitten, you’ll dish out between $1,000 to $16,000 and beyond.

Fishing cats are a breed of wild cats found in Southeast Asia. They’ve undergone no domestication, so they aren’t traditional pets. You have to have special permits or licenses to own a Fishing cat, and they certainly aren’t legal in every state. Expect to pay between $1,000 and $20,000–depending on age and quality.
